## 1. Additions to Lecture 4 (Dynamic Programming & Calculus of Optimal Control)

### 1b. LQR as a Single QP (CMU Lec 8)
- **What to take**: Formulating finite-horizon LQR as one big quadratic program, the KKT system structure, and showing how the Riccati recursion emerges from block-eliminating the KKT system.
- **Why**: Current notes derive Riccati via DP only. The QP/KKT perspective gives a second derivation that connects LQR to constrained optimization (Lecture 3) and explains the sparsity structure exploited in practice.
- **Placement**: Could fit in Lecture 6 (LQR) as an alternative derivation subsection, or in Lecture 4 to show the DP-KKT connection.
- **CMU source**: Lec 8, Subsections 1-3

### 1c. PMP-DP Fundamental Connection (CMU Lec 9)
- **What to take**: Explicit proof/discussion showing costate = gradient of value function, unifying PMP and DP.
- **Why**: Current notes mention the connection (Sec 4.4 remark) but the CMU notes dedicate a subsection to it. A more thorough treatment would strengthen the theory.
- **Placement**: End of Lecture 4, after both PMP and HJB are presented.
- **CMU source**: Lec 9, Subsection 5

## 3. Additions to Lecture 3 (Constrained Optimization)

### 3a. Interior-Point Methods (CMU Lec 5)
- **What to take**: Barrier functions, central path, interior-point algorithm for inequality-constrained problems.
- **Why**: Current notes cover KKT and duality but stop before discussing how to actually solve inequality-constrained problems. Interior-point methods are the primary solver behind modern trajectory optimization.
- **Placement**: New subsection after Section 3.5 (Lagrangian Duality) or Section 3.6 (QP).
- **CMU source**: Lec 5, Subsection 3

### 3b. Merit Functions and Line Search for Constrained Problems (CMU Lec 6)
- **What to take**: Merit functions (L1 penalty, augmented Lagrangian), line search in the presence of constraints.
- **Why**: Bridges the gap between the KKT theory (Lecture 3) and the NLP solvers used in trajectory optimization (Lecture 5).
- **Placement**: End of Lecture 3 or beginning of Lecture 5.
- **CMU source**: Lec 6, Subsection 3

### 3c. KKT System Regularization (CMU Lec 6)
- **What to take**: Inertia correction, regularization of saddle-point KKT systems.
- **Why**: Practical detail needed for understanding how NLP solvers handle degenerate/near-singular KKT systems.
- **Placement**: Subsection in Lecture 3 after QP section.
- **CMU source**: Lec 6, Subsection 2

## 5. Entirely New Topics (New Lectures or Appendix Sections)

### 5a. Attitude Control and Quaternion Optimization (CMU Lec 13-15)
- **What to take**:
  - Rotation representations (rotation matrices SO(3), unit quaternions S^3)
  - Quaternion kinematics
  - Error-state linearization and the attitude Jacobian
  - LQR on manifolds (projection matrix E(x) for tangent space)
  - 3D quadrotor dynamics and attitude control

- **Why**: Important for any robotics application. Shows how optimal control extends to non-Euclidean state spaces. Not covered at all in current notes.
- **Placement**: New lecture or appendix chapter on "Optimal Control on Manifolds" or "Attitude Control".
